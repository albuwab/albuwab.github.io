My Journey

Considering how I can help tell your story? Allow me to tell you a bit about mine so far. 

Originally from a small city in Arkansas, I attended Harding University and graduated Suma Cum Laude with a degree in public relations in three years. A few weeks after my graduation, I had another major life celebration - my wedding. That took me to Oklahoma where my husband was finishing school at the University of Oklahoma. During that time, I worked briefly at the university and then joined a statewide nonprofit in Oklahoma City, the Oklahoma Institute for Child Advocacy (OICA). I served as an AmeriCorps member at OICA eventually taking on the role of Communications Coordinator but focused mostly on the KIDS Count Initiative. I worked directly with our Advocacy/Policy Director, often joining her at the State Capitol throughout legislative session. I assisted with maintaining OICA's website, managed its social media accounts, created newsletters, designed infographics and delivered advocacy presentations at special events.


After my husband graduated and I completed my AmeriCorps service term, the job market brought us to DFW. I was hired at a PR firm in Dallas and worked for three years there as an account executive on multiple client accounts. Some clients were in state; others were spread across the country - Florida, Indiana, California and even Washington. This job gave me invaluable experiences and developed my media relations skills in ways I could not have imagined. I always loved the writing process invovled for the press releases and traveling to be onsite whenever possible. I was afforded the opportunity to interview impressive experts in different industries and craft profile pieces about incredible individuals from published authors to remarkable veterans . I also had the chance to work with fascinating individuals at events planned by the firm including Jane Seymour, Jack Hanna, Henry Winkler, Carl Bernstein, Frank Abagnale, Jane Pauley and Coach Bob Knight.


Now I"ve been enjoying freelance work for clients in the form of different projects based on individual needs.

My Why

More than a job

I am a writer at heart. I am passionate about the written word. Clear, open, effective communication is becoming a dying art. Countless messages are miscommunicated or never communicated leading to misinterpretations and false presuppositions and/or missed opportunities. That can mean damaged or lost relationships, which is extremely bothersome to an extrovert, like myself.


I enjoy building relationships, discovering others' stories and helping convey ideas to connect people. I believe in my core that we are all relational beings, and if we took the time to get to know ourselves and get to know each other better by communicating well, we could avoid numerous misunderstandings and conflicts. Whether you're trying to raise awareness about a deeply personal cause, or you're attempting to get the word out about your business or product, a story is waiting to be shared. Communicating that story to the right group of people at the right time in order to reach them is work. And that's the kind of work I do.


When I am not working, I am watching Ted Talks and reading about a myriad of topics from communication strategies to brand development, catching a webinar on social media scheduling and catching up on news articles regarding local happenings and international affairs. I also like to check out the social documentaries on Netflix. Health-related articles usually dominate my personal social media news feeds with natural food recipes as I genuinely like to cook. I practice yoga, love rock climbing and enjoy exploring various workout routines. Realizing improvements are constant necessities in all aspects of life, I make an effort to set S.M.A.R.T. (Specific Measurable, Attainable, Realistic, Time-bound) goals and give my best in order to reach those goals.
